# Mini-Hexaplex Analysis Report

## Purpose

This test asks whether short six-strand mini-hexaplex coordinate truncations show the radial/diffraction features seen in the full-length model, and specifically whether 12 base/GLU units per strand recover more of the full-length 4.5-5.0 A signal than the previously analyzed 8-unit truncations.

This is a sensitivity and compatibility analysis only. It does not determine the true structure.

## Baseline and convention

- Baseline full-length structure: outputs/intermediates/ai_candidate_inputs/full_hexaplex_anti_parallel_30deg_ideal_deduped_6chain.pdb
- Variant manifest: outputs/mini_hexaplex/mini_hexaplex_variant_manifest.csv
- Geometry sanity table: outputs/metrics/mini_hexaplex_geometry_summary.csv
- Analysis mode: fiber
- Unit convention reminder: q = 2*pi/d.
- Full cleaned baseline length: 15 base/GLU units per chain, 30 residues per chain, 180 residues total.
- The 8-unit mini-hexaplex models were already run and are retained here for comparison when present.
- A base/GLU repeat unit is one CYP/GLU or MEP/GLU pair in residue order.

## First-N ambiguity

The six strands are anti-parallel or overlapping from different physical ends, so the first N residues or units in chain order do not necessarily correspond to the same end of the hexaplex. The literal firstN variant is therefore a sequence-order control and may not be a compact physical segment. The lower-end and central variants use fitted-axis coordinates and are the more physically meaningful truncations.

## Variants

- `literal_firstN_units`: first N base/GLU repeat units by residue order in every chain.
- `lower_end_firstN_units`: N base/GLU repeat units closest to the lower fitted-axis coordinate end in every chain.
- `centralN_units`: N base/GLU repeat units nearest the fitted-axis center.

Coordinates, residue names, atom names, and chain IDs are preserved from the baseline. Residue numbering is preserved; output atom serials are regenerated by the repo PDB writer.

## Conservative interpretation

- 4.5-5.0 A feature: the short variants have nonzero window signal but no conservative local-maximum call.
- The 3.4 A reference window has nonzero intensity in all analyzed mini variants.
- The 3.0 A reference window has nonzero intensity in all analyzed mini variants.
- The 4.5-5.0 A integrated intensity by truncation rule: 12-unit variants are very similar across truncation definitions; 8-unit variants are very similar across truncation definitions.
- 12-unit comparison: 12-unit variants are included; see ratio_to_full_length_4p5_5A and comparison_to_8unit_models for whether they strengthen relative to 8-unit variants.
- Any conclusion about whether longer strand length is necessary should be read from the comparative feature scores above, not from the coordinate truncation alone.

## Limitations

- These mini models are coordinate truncations, not independently relaxed or minimized structures.
- Truncation may introduce end effects.
- The workflow includes no solvent, counterions, or remodeling.
- The diffraction simulation remains idealized.
- Emory data are oriented/fiber-like, not random powder.
- Raw experimental radial data are needed for stronger conclusions.
