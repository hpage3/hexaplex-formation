# Hexaplex Formation Hypotheses

## H1: Local peptide-pair interactions are insufficient

Isolated beta-strand / alanine-pair controls should not reproduce the structured 4–6 Å diffraction features seen in the Hexaplex scaffold.

Status: partly supported by central 5-alanine controls, which produce smoother radial profiles.

## H2: The d ≈ 4.5 Å feature is a Hexaplex scaffold signature

The d ≈ 4.5 Å feature differentiates the organized Hexaplex scaffold from generic isolated peptide-pair controls.

This feature should be treated as a reciprocal-space signature of repeated folded/twisted scaffold geometry, not as a direct real-space 4.5 Å contact.

## H3: Helical order emerges at a multi-strand threshold

The helical Hexaplex shape may not appear in isolated peptide pairs or two-strand fragments. It may emerge only when three or more scaffold segments begin to close around the assembly.

Possible threshold models:

- isolated peptide pair
- two-strand scaffold fragment
- three-strand pre-helix
- four-strand partial scaffold
- six-strand closed scaffold
- scaffold + stacked hexads

## H4: Hexad stacking and scaffold closure are cooperative

The d ≈ 3.3–3.4 Å feature is mainly associated with stacked hexads, while the 4–6 Å structured features are associated with scaffold organization.

The final Hexaplex may require cooperative interaction between hexad stacking and scaffold closure.

## H5: GLU-rich motif repetition contributes to scaffold organization

Pair-attribution results suggest repeated GLU-rich residue-pair relationships contribute constructively near q-values corresponding to d ≈ 4–5 Å.

These may reflect repeated scaffold geometry, backbone/side-chain oxygen patterns, hydrogen bonding, ion/water mediation, steric complementarity, or cooperative closure.

Direct GLU–GLU electrostatic attraction should not be assumed without protonation, ion, or water context.
